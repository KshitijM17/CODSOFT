3. Weather Station (Using Node MCU)

In this project, I engineered a Weather Station that seamlessly integrates cutting-edge technologies to capture, analyze, and present real-time weather data. The core components include a NodeMCU microcontroller, a DHT22 sensor for precise temperature and humidity measurements, and the utilization of ThingSpeak cloud platform for data storage and visualization. Additionally, the system incorporates a DC fan for environmental control based on the acquired weather data. The NodeMCU, serving as the project's brain, orchestrates the collection of weather data through the DHT22 sensor, ensuring accurate and reliable measurements. These data points are then transmitted to the ThingSpeak cloud, creating a centralized hub for comprehensive data analysis and visualization. To enhance the practical application of the weather station, I integrated a DC fan into the system. This fan can be autonomously controlled based on the real-time weather conditions, providing a dynamic response to temperature and humidity changes. For instance, the fan may be activated to cool down the environment if the temperature rises above a certain threshold. This project showcases my proficiency in Internet of Things (IoT) applications, sensor integration, cloud-based data management, and the implementation of responsive control systems. The Weather Station not only serves as a testament to my technical skills but also highlights the potential for creating smart and adaptive solutions in the field of environmental monitoring.

Autonomous V tail Plane

In a recent project, I successfully designed and implemented an Autonomous V-Tail Drone, incorporating advanced features for autopilot mode using an MPU6050 accelerometer and gyroscope sensor. This system ensures precise direction control and acts as a failsafe mechanism, triggering autopilot mode if the drone goes out of the range of the transmitter. The MPU6050 is seamlessly interfaced with an Arduino, and servo motors are employed for control surface manipulation. The MPU6050 sensor plays a pivotal role in the project, providing real-time data on the drone's orientation. This data is then processed by the Arduino to make dynamic adjustments to the servo motors, allowing the drone to maintain stability and control in various flight conditions. In the event of the drone going beyond the transmitter range, the autopilot mode is activated, leveraging the MPU6050 data to guide the drone safely back to a predefined location. To cater to manual control, an external controller is integrated to provide the pilot with a user-friendly interface. This allows the drone to be manually operated when needed, offering flexibility and control during different phases of flight. This project not only showcases my expertise in sensor integration and microcontroller programming but also emphasizes the implementation of failsafe mechanisms for enhanced safety and autonomous control. The Autonomous V-Tail Drone serves as a testament to my ability to develop sophisticated aerial systems with both autonomous and manual control capabilities.
